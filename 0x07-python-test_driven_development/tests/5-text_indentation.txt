The ``text_indentation`` module

``text_indentation``  prints a text with 2 new 
lines after each of these characters: ., ? and :

Import text_indentation:
	>>> text_indentation = __import__('5-text_indentation').text_indentation

# Test Case 1 text_indentation with string:
	>>> text_indentation("I am Successful")
	I am Successful

	>>> text_indentation("pharmacy.School")
	pharmacy.
	<BLANKLINE>
	School

	>>> text_indentation("Ahmadu Bello. School? Are you still in school: Bolad")
	Ahmadu Bello.
	<BLANKLINE>
	School?
	<BLANKLINE>
	Are you still in school:
	<BLANKLINE>
	Bolad

# Test Case 2: text_indentation with non-string:
	>>> text_indentation(123)
